i-mAB — Integrated machine learning pipeline for aberrant biomarker enrichment: characterizing clusters of differentiation in SLE

Trang T. Le · Nigel O. Blackwood · Jaclyn N. Taroni · Weixuan Fu · Matthew K. Breitenstein  ·  Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania
↗ i-mAB on GitHub Pages ← CD Annotation Preprint Source Code

Clusters of differentiation (CDs) are cell surface biomarkers shaped by upstream, interdependent biological features. Within a compendium of SLE patients, i-mAB applied relief-based machine learning (MultiSURF) to identify 681 predictive gene expression features, then used elastic net regularized regression to profile de novo genes affecting aberrant expression of CD20, CD22, and CD30 — three CDs with FDA-approved -mab therapeutics in B-lymphocyte malignancies and autoimmune disease. Key associations were replicated in an independent cell line model system.

Motivation

B-cell surface antigens are the molecular targets of a growing class of therapeutic monoclonal antibodies — drugs whose names end in -mab (monoclonal antibody) or -lib (library-derived). CD20, CD22, and CD30 were selected because each is characteristically overexpressed in B-lymphocyte hematologic malignancies and autoimmune disorders, and each has FDA-approved targeted therapies that have demonstrated trans-disease repositioning potential.

However, CDs are not isolated — they are shaped by upstream, correlated gene expression networks. Standard regression struggles under multicollinearity. The i-mAB pipeline was purpose-built to navigate this complexity, using algorithms robust to class imbalance and correlated predictors, to enrich the perspective of CD biology with de novo transcriptomic features.

Data

The SLE Compendium aggregated human gene expression data from 6 GEO studies, normalized using SCAN (Affymetrix) and quantile normalization (Agilent/Illumina), scaled 0–1 per gene. The compendium contained 15,497 gene expression features across 1,576 patient observations — 160 healthy controls, 1,290 treatment-naïve SLE, and 126 SLE with treatment exposure. Full preprocessing code is available via the Greene Lab GitHub.

Four-stage pipeline
🗂️
Stage A
SLE Compendium assembly
Six GEO datasets aggregated, SCAN-normalized (Affymetrix) or quantile-normalized (Agilent/Illumina), and scaled 0–1 per gene. Healthy controls, treatment-naïve SLE, and treatment-exposed SLE retained. All CDs annotated as aberrant/non-aberrant using 20th/80th percentile two-tailed cutoffs (detailed in companion preprint).
📊 1,576 observations · 15,497 gene features
🎯
Stage B — Feature selection 1
MultiSURF: identifying SLE-predictive genes
MultiSURF, a relief-based algorithm robust to class imbalance and gene-gene interactions, was applied to 80% training data (CDs excluded to prevent circularity). Features were ranked by importance score; a heuristic threshold of 0.177 selected the most discriminating genes. TPOT (automated ML) validated the selected features via an optimized stacked pipeline of gradient boosting, decision tree, and Random Forest.
🧬 681 predictive genes selected
⚖️ Balanced accuracy 0.822
κ Kappa 0.680
🔬
Stage C — Feature selection 2
Elastic net: CD-specific gene expression profiles
Within the 681 stage-B features, elastic net (α=0.5, λ tuned by cross-validation) performed regularized logistic regression separately for each CD aberrance label. L1+L2 penalties simultaneously selected and shrunk correlated predictors. Retained genes were then independently tested via univariate logistic regression to report odds ratios and 95% CIs.
CD20 k=53 genes, D=0.0698
CD22 k=78 genes, D=0.1351
CD30 k=137 genes, D=0.1908
🌐
Stages D–E
Pathway enrichment + independent replication
GSEA (Reactome + GO molecular function) annotated CD-associated gene sets. An independent panel of 64 human-derived cell lines from the Human Protein Atlas provided disease-agnostic in silico replication via correlation testing of transcript counts against CD expression. P-values adjusted using Benjamini-Hochberg FDR.

PHOSPHO1 — a shared signal

PHOSPHO1 is a phosphatase linked to bone mineralization and phosphocholine/phosphoethanolamine metabolism. Its association with both CD22 and CD30 expression — replicated in independent cell lines — implicates phosphate-containing compound metabolic pathways as a shared upstream biological context for these two B-cell surface markers. Future in vitro study is warranted to elucidate the mechanistic basis of phosphate signaling on aberrant CD22 and CD30 expression.

Limitations & generalizability

Aggregation of public datasets minimizes cost but may introduce cohort-level batch effects; a potential origin effect was observed for GSE49454/GSE61635 in the CD30 regression. A subset of "treatment-naïve" patients received maintenance immunosuppressive therapy, which may have slightly attenuated the transcriptome. Additionally, some proteins degrade rapidly while others persist — elevated transcript levels may be required to produce comparable bioavailable protein in some cases, complicating direct CD expression inference. Replication in tissue-specific environments and additional disease contexts is warranted.
