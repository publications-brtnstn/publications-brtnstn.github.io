Background

This manuscript serves as an extended annotation of gene expression for all known clusters of differentiation (CDs) within a compendium of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) patients. The overarching goal is to enrich the perspective of the CD transcriptome with upstream gene expression features.

CDs are cell surface biomarkers that denote key biological differences between cell types and disease state. For each of the >400 known CDs, distinct monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) enable robust immunophenotyping and serve as scalable biomarkers for translational research — including the growing landscape of -mab and -lib therapeutics targeting these surface antigens.

CD nomenclature is organized through the Human Leucocyte Differentiation Antigens (HLDA) Workshops, affiliated with the Human Cell Differentiation Molecules (HCDM) organization.

Classification pipeline
1
SLE Compendium construction
Human SLE gene expression data collected, aggregated, and normalized. Modified to include demographic data and restrict to healthy control, treatment-naïve SLE, and treated SLE observations.
2
Two-tailed normalization
CD expression labeled aberrant (low/high) or non-aberrant using 20th and 80th percentile thresholds as default cutoffs across all 351 identified CD genes.
3
Manual threshold adjustment
Consensus review applied for CDs near-normally distributed but requiring slight threshold shifts (n=3 adjusted) to better capture feature variation.
4
Binarization for non-normal distributions
CDs with clearly non-normal distributions (n=85) binarized into low/high groups rather than aberrant/non-aberrant, capturing apparent expression patterns without a spurious baseline comparison.
5
i-mAB pipeline (future work)
CDs will be enriched with interdependent gene expression features using the integrated machine learning pipeline for aberrant biomarker enrichment.
