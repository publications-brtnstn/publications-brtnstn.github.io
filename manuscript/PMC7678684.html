Perspective · 2020 · Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics

Translational Informatics Connects Real-World Information to Knowledge in an Increasingly Data-Driven World

McDonough, Breitenstein et al. Clin Pharmacol Ther · Epub 2020 DOI: 10.1002/cpt.1719
Read on Wiley ↗ Cite this manuscript

Real-world data is only as powerful as the methods that interpret it. Translational informatics is the discipline that closes the gap — from zettabytes of clinical and genomic data to actionable knowledge at the bedside.

This perspective, written for the Translational Informatics community within ASCPT, argues that the convergence of machine learning, EHRs, multi-omics, and common data models has created an unprecedented opportunity to advance drug discovery and patient-centered care. The piece maps the bidirectional interfaces — from bench to bedside and back — that define the translational cycle, and calls on the informatics community to bridge existing gaps in clinical pharmacology and translational medicine.

Abstract

The clinical pharmacology community stands on the cusp of a data-empowered revolution, where health data artifacts ascertained from real-world data sources — including electronic health records, patient wearables, genomics, and other 'omics — hold momentous opportunity to inform our understanding of patient outcomes. The opportunity to advance our molecular understanding of why select patients do not respond to certain therapies in larger, more representative populations will empower future drug discovery and facilitate research.

Themes

01

Big data & drug discovery

How machine learning applied to TCGA, UK Biobank, and multi-omics repositories accelerates target identification and drug repurposing.

02

EHR as research substrate

Computable phenotypes, natural language processing, and the challenge of extracting drug response and adverse events from unstructured clinical notes.

03

Real-world data sources

Patient registries, wearables, claims data, direct-to-consumer genomics, and the Million Veterans Program as pharmacogenomic discovery engines.

04

Common data models

PCORnet, OMOP, and the ACT Network — enabling cross-institutional cohort discovery across more than 40 million patients in real time.

05

Patient-centered research

All of Us, Fitbit data, and the emerging expectation of result return — integrating patient-provided data streams into discovery pipelines.

06

Translational cycle

Bidirectional interfaces between data, information, knowledge, and clinical decision — the full loop from population to patient and back.
