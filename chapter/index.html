Fundamentals of Drug Metabolism and Pharmacogenomics Within a Learning Healthcare System Workflow Perspective

Breitenstein & Crowgey In: Personalized and Precision Medicine Informatics pp. 59–80 922 accesses
Read on Springer ↗ Cite this chapter

Pharmacogenomics is only as useful as the system that delivers it. This chapter frames the field through the lens of a learning healthcare system — where clinical data feeds the model, and the model feeds the clinic.

The learning healthcare system is a framework in which evidence is continuously generated from real-world patient data and fed back into clinical practice. Pharmacogenomics — the study of how genetic variation shapes drug response — is a natural fit for this loop. Our chapter traces the informatics workflow end-to-end: from genotype to actionable clinical recommendation, and from clinical outcomes back to knowledge advancement.

Abstract

This chapter introduces fundamental concepts of pharmacogenomics, including drug metabolism, and provides an informatics workflow-based perspective inspired by a learning healthcare system framework. Our intent is that the reader sees pharmacogenomics as a foundation of precision medicine, which is reliant upon informatics to deliver actionable patient-tailored knowledge at the point of care. Further, pharmacogenomics knowledge is poised to be further developed so as to be amenable to multi-drug comorbid disease treatment necessitated by precision medicine practice. The methodologic considerations highlighted within this workflow-based perspective encompass end-to-end forward and reverse translational informatics activities, designed to both appropriately deploy existing pharmacogenomics knowledge, as well as contribute to its advancement by harnessing insights from real-world data.

The loop

→

Forward translation

Genotype data is processed through NGS pipelines, annotated against resources like PharmGKB and CPIC, and delivered as actionable pharmacogenomic guidance at the point of care.

→

Clinical deployment

Patient-tailored drug-gene interactions surface within the EHR workflow — informing prescribing decisions for multi-drug, comorbid disease scenarios in precision medicine practice.

↩

Reverse translation

Real-world outcomes are captured, phenotyped from the EHR, and fed back into the model — closing the loop and expanding the pharmacogenomics knowledge base.
